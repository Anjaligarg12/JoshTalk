Introduction

An overview of generative artificial intelligence and its application to traditional Indian garments in digital retail.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has significantly transformed digital content creation, especially in the field of AI Image Generation. Modern text-to-image models can generate high-quality images from simple text prompts, making them valuable for industries such as E-commerce, fashion, marketing, and digital advertising.

India has one of the world's fastest-growing E-commerce markets, where high-quality product images play an essential role in influencing customer purchasing decisions. AI-generated product photography has the potential to reduce production costs, accelerate content creation, and improve scalability. However, the quality of generated images varies across different AI models.

This evaluation focuses on Indian Ethnic Clothing, a category that represents India's cultural diversity and is widely sold across online shopping platforms. The objective of this study is to compare multiple AI image generation models and identify which model produces the most realistic, culturally accurate, and E-commerce-ready product images.

The evaluation is based on Human Evaluation, where participants compare generated images using predefined criteria such as prompt accuracy, realism, visual appeal, cultural representation, and suitability for E-commerce platforms.

Problem Statement

Challenges in Evaluating AI-Generated Indian Ethnic Clothing Images

Indian e-commerce platforms increasingly rely on AI-generated product images to reduce production costs and improve content creation speed. However, different AI models produce inconsistent results in realism, clothing details, cultural representation, lighting, and image quality.

Inconsistent Image Quality Different AI models generate varying levels of realism and detail.
Cultural Representation Some models fail to accurately represent Indian ethnic clothing, fabrics, embroidery, jewelry, and styling.
E-commerce Readiness Images may look attractive but still be unsuitable for product listings because of incorrect poses, unrealistic garments, or distorted clothing.
Lack of Standard Evaluation There is no simple human-centered evaluation framework focused specifically on Indian ethnic fashion.
AI-to-Retail Synthesis Flow
Step 01 Prompt Design Inputting clothing specifications, fabric textures, demographics, and studio settings.
Step 02 Model Generation AI model processing of latent features to render high-resolution portrait imagery.
Step 03 Cultural Validation Verifying drape physics, pattern details, facial alignments, and clothing accuracy.
Step 04 Catalog Listing Integrating approved generated model photography into active e-commerce storefronts.
Why This Matters

Selecting the right AI model can improve Customer Trust, reduce photography costs by up to 80%, accelerate content creation speed, and help Indian e-commerce businesses Scale Efficiently in a highly competitive digital market.

Key Evaluation Insights
  • Prompt Adherence Dominance: GPT Image 1 displayed exceptional compliance with prompts detailing intricate regional garments, accurately stitching specified zari borders and thread textures.
  • Texture & Realism Split: While GPT won overall, Gemini 2.5 Flash Preview outperformed other models in soft fabric draping, natural skin tones, folds, and accessory/jewelry detailing.
  • Latency vs. Fidelity Tradeoff: Gemini 2.5 Flash achieved rapid generation times but suffered in quality, demonstrating simplified patterns and minor pixelation on complex traditional textures.
  • Demographic Sensitivity: Evaluators with high familiarity ("Yes") graded models significantly stricter, noticing minor drape inconsistencies and jewelry misalignments that general users missed.
  • E-commerce Commercial Readiness: Studio lighting, balanced contrast, and clear white backgrounds in GPT Image 1 aligned closest with target commercial catalog benchmarks.
Summary of Participant Feedback

Participants noted that GPT Image 1 had the most stable designs and is "e-commerce ready" with clear embroidery patterns. Gemini Preview was highly appreciated for its "stunning realism" and "natural fabric folds." Gemini Flash was praised for speed but critiqued for "simplified patterns."

Final Conclusion

GPT Image 1 stands as the commercial benchmark for e-commerce cataloging due to its robust prompt alignment and high studio-style appeal. However, Gemini Preview represents the future of high-realism fabric simulation if semantic prompt mapping is refined.

Strategic approach for scaling human evaluation workflows from a small-scale cohort to 10,000+ participants.

From 10 to 10,000+ Participants: Transition from manual/local sharing to a distributed crowdsourcing platform (e.g., MTurk, Toloka) integrated with global panels. Deploy microservices on Google Cloud/Firebase to auto-balance loads and manage concurrent ratings.

Reflection

Personal reflections on the evaluation process, its local relevance, and core learnings.

Why This Evaluation?
Reason for Selection: Text-to-image models are famously poor at capturing regional textiles, jewelry styles, and intricate drapes (e.g., saree pleats, anarkali folds). Traditional automated metrics (like FID) fail to evaluate cultural alignment, making human feedback the only reliable option.
Usefulness for India: India's e-commerce market is heavily fashion-driven. Generative AI cataloging can cut catalog photography costs by 90% for local sellers, but only if the generated imagery is culturally accurate and respectful.
Relevance to AI Labs Building for India

AI labs building Indian foundation models must invest in localized evaluation datasets. Evaluating models on generic Western benchmarks (like MS-COCO) will miss cultural inaccuracies in attire, regional backgrounds, and festivals, leading to poor product adoption.

Key Personal Learnings
  • Multi-Dimensional Divergence: A model can achieve extreme photorealism but fail completely at draping a saree correctly. Evaluating across single dimensions is misleading.
  • Stabilizing Subjectivity: Human feedback is highly subjective. Creating structured, dimension-specific star rubrics is crucial to maintain statistical stability and consistency across scorers.
  • E-commerce Aesthetic Standards: Shadows, lighting consistency, and background cleanliness matter just as much as dress details when assessing retail readiness.
Future Technical Enhancements

If given more time, I would build a pre-screening pipeline utilizing Vision-Language Models (like Gemini 1.5 Flash) to automatically filter out outputs with obvious anatomical errors or fabric distortions before routing them to human evaluators.

Reflection Summary

Human-in-the-loop evaluation is not a temporary phase, but a permanent, vital component of localized AI generation pipelines, ensuring that visual models honor cultural nuances while meeting commercial standards.
